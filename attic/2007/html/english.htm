How to Join the NTNUI Swimming Team

Requirements not controlled by NTNUI demand that all members of the NTNUI swimming team hold a valid license with the Norwegian Swimming Association (NSF) in order to take part in our scheduled training activities. The situation is explained in more detail below.

How to Obtain a Valid License

There are two cases:

  • Swimmers registering during the autumn semester

    An agreement between NTNUI, the Trondheim city administration and Pirbadet Foundation stipulates that new and existing members of the NTNUI swimming team make an advance payment, equal to the NSF license fee, to the swimming team's account. A bulk payment encompassing all accumulated amounts of the autumn semester is subsequently, at the start of next year, made to the NSF.

    Through this arrangement, you will not have a valid NSF license for the current year unless you already hold one. The only consequence of this is that you will not be allowed to enter any NSF approved competitions in the autum semester. You will, on the other hand, obtain a valid license for the entirety of next year.

  • Swimmers registering during the spring semester

    You should make a payment equal to the NSF license fee to the swimming group's account. The money will be expeditiously forwarded by the board of the NTNUI swimming team to NSF's license and registration office.

In either case, you will of course be allowed to take part in scheduled training activities from the moment your payment is registered on our account. The NSF license fee is currently NOK 330, and this amount should be paid to the Swimming group's account 5361.06.92571 of "DNBnor". Electronic payments made through online banking are fully accepted. Please allow some days for transaction processing by the bank and ensure that your name is associated with the transaction.

The means by which the payment is made are immaterial, but we would like to stress the benefits of having a written receipt of the transaction in case any trouble occurs. Such a receipt is most easily attained by using a "giro" which should be addressed to

The above information is essential to member registration and the NSF, and no-one will be registered as a member prior to providing this information. We furthermore ask you to hold on to your receipt at least until your name is included in the member list at http://www.redacted-domain.com/svomming/html/medlemsliste.php.

Finally, once your name shows up in the member list, there is another administrative task you will have to perform. Pirbadet will provide you with a specially designed admittance chip. The chip is available at the counter at Pirbadet and you will have to pay a deposit of NOK 100. You should do this no later than 30 minutes before starting your first training session with the swimming team. You will also need a NOK 10 coin to lock the locker door each time you visit Pirbadet, but this coin is returned when you unlock the locker.

Why We Demand a Valid NSF License

In the summer of 2001 our previous training facilities at Sentralbadet were closed permanently and the swimming team's activities were moved to the, at the time, recently opened swimming pool at Pirbadet. The Pirbadet Foundation responsible for running the latter, together with local authorities financially sponsoring our training, maintain that only dedicated swimmers be allowed to participate in our scheduled training hours.

By an, in our opinion, somewhat contrived definition, a "dedicated" swimmer holds a valid license with the NSF and this equates to all of our members having to hold such a license.

On a final note: The swimming team rents training space in the main pool only. As a consequence, your membership in the swimming team does not permit you to use other facilities in Pirbadet during our training sessions. These facilities may be enjoyed during regular hours at Pirbadet but you will, of course, have to pay standard admission fees to do so.
